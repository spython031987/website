Understanding safety, risks and harm-benefit balances of home-based care

Overview of the Study

The SAFE@HOME study examines the concept of safety within the context of home-based care, recognising that care delivered in the home involves unique risks, responsibilities, and interpersonal dynamics. At present, there is limited clarity around how safety, risk, and potential benefits or harms should be defined and assessed in this setting. Without a well-developed conceptual framework, it becomes difficult to identify when service failures occur, understand their consequences, or implement meaningful improvements.

By analysing how safety is understood and experienced by individuals involved in caregiving, the study aims to establish clearer definitions and measurement approaches. Strengthening this conceptual foundation will support more accurate evaluation of care outcomes and inform changes to practice, service design, and policy. Ultimately, the study seeks to enhance the reliability and safety of home-based care and improve protections for both care recipients and caregivers.

Research Scope and Focus

Research Question

What does good home-based care look like when we consider the real risks, challenges, and harms experienced by people receiving care and their families?

Research Aim

To develop a clear and practical understanding of what "safety" means in home-based careâ€”one that reflects real-time decisions about risks and benefits and supports a system designed around what truly matters to people receiving care.

Research Objectives

  • Review existing research to identify key ideas and frameworks that help define and evaluate safe care in the home setting.
  • Analyse safety incidents reported by the social care workforce to understand where problems occur and how services can be improved.
  • Examine the factors that support or hinder safe care, including the relationships and practices across different professional groups, social care organisations, and health and care providers.
  • Explore the experiences of care recipients and their families, including their concerns about safety and how they navigate care at home.

Study Design

This mixed-methods realist study aims to develop a clear and comprehensive understanding of what safety means in personalised home-based care for older adults. By examining how risks and benefits are balanced in everyday situations, the study seeks to inform a care system that better responds to what matters most to people receiving care.

Work Packages

Work Package 1 (WP1)

A review of existing research and evidence to understand how safe home-based care is defined and supported.

Work Package 2a (WP2a)

An analysis of safety incidents reported by the social care workforce to Care Inspectorate Wales, helping us understand where problems occur in home-based care.

Work Package 2b (WP2b)

Focus groups and interviews with professionals involved in providing or supporting home-based care.

Work Package 3 (WP3)

Interviews with people receiving care at home and with their informal or unpaid caregivers to learn about their experiences and concerns.

Stakeholder Events

Three stakeholder events will bring together professionals, service users, and the public to help interpret the findings and co-produce practical solutions.

Oversight Group

An independent Oversight Group (OG) has been appointed to provide impartial advice, guidance, and oversight throughout the study. The group supports delivery of the study in line with appropriate standards of rigour and ethical conduct. The study team consults the OG regarding any significant changes to the study design or methods, and meetings are held every six months. The OG operates according to a members' charter that outlines the roles and responsibilities of the group.

Research Management Group

All co-applicants of the study are members of the Research Management Group (RMG), including public contributors (SH, RK, and MCH). Meetings take place quarterly to oversee project progress both locally and across all sites. ACS leads and coordinates timescales, methodological input, data collection, analysis, interpretation, reporting, and dissemination, with day-to-day support from the study data manager (Saptarshi Putatunda). Data specification, extraction, and management of routine data are undertaken by the data manager. RMG meetings take place quarterly.

Task and Finish Groups

Task and Finish (T&F) Groups were established to complete specific tasks within each phase of the study. Individuals with specialist skills are co-opted onto T&F Groups as appropriate, including public contributors. These meetings are held every two to four weeks.

Public Advisory Panel

A Public Advisory Panel of nine membersâ€”including our three public co-applicants, Ms SH, Mr RK, and Ms MC was convened. This group provides an important forum for discussion and advice on study conduct and communication, ensuring that the patient and public perspective remains central to the study.

Patient & Public Involvement

Public contributors play a vital role in the SAFE@HOME study, serving as co-applicants and members of the Public Advisory Panel. Their involvement ensures that the patient and public perspective remains central to all aspects of the study, from design through to dissemination.

Frequently Asked Questions

We are looking to speak with individuals who have experience of receiving or providing home-based care for older adults. This may include:

  • Older adults currently receiving home-based care
  • Family members or informal caregivers supporting older adults at home
  • Professional care workers delivering home-based care services
  • Healthcare professionals involved in supporting home-based care

Specific eligibility criteria may vary depending on which work package you are invited to participate in. The study team will provide detailed information about eligibility when recruitment begins.

The SAFE@HOME study takes data protection very seriously. Your privacy and confidentiality are paramount:

  • Confidentiality: All information collected will be kept strictly confidential and stored securely in accordance with UK data protection legislation (GDPR and Data Protection Act 2018).
  • Anonymisation: Your personal information will be removed from research data, and you will be assigned a unique study number. Data will be anonymised before analysis.
  • Secure Storage: All data will be stored on secure, password-protected Cardiff University systems with access restricted to authorized research team members only.
  • Data Retention: Research data will be retained in accordance with Cardiff University's research data management policy and relevant regulatory requirements.
  • Your Rights: You have the right to access your data, request corrections, or withdraw from the study at any time without giving a reason.

Further details about data handling will be provided in the Participant Information Sheet.

Participation will depend on which aspect of the study you are involved in:

  • Interviews: You may be invited to participate in a one-to-one interview with a member of the research team. Interviews typically last 45-60 minutes and can be conducted in person, by telephone, or via video call, depending on your preference.
  • Focus Groups: Some participants may be invited to join a focus group discussion with other participants. These usually last around 90 minutes.
  • Follow-up: In some cases, we may ask if you would be willing to participate in a brief follow-up conversation to clarify or expand on information provided.

All participation is voluntary, and you can withdraw at any time without any consequences. The Participant Information Sheet will provide full details about what your participation would involve.

We recognize that participating in research takes time and effort. While specific details will be provided in the Participant Information Sheet, we typically offer:

  • Reasonable travel expenses for any face-to-face meetings
  • Recognition of your time contribution in accordance with NIHR INVOLVE guidance
  • Refreshments during face-to-face meetings

The study team will provide full details about any compensation when you are invited to participate.
